🦋 Build a Giant Flapping Butterfly

Step 1 of 10
1

Pick Your Size

📏
  • Go big! A few 6-8 ft butterflies make way more impact than lots of little ones
  • Small (3 ft) is great for a first try or kids' project
  • Medium (6 ft) and Large (8 ft) look stunning at the gala

Size picked? Awesome! Everything else follows from here.

🎯 Wing Lab: Tap the Small, Medium, or Large preset button — it loads all recommended measurements instantly Open Wing Lab →
2

Pick Your Materials

🛒

Our top recommendation for the gala:

  • Bamboo stakes for the wing frame (light, strong, cheap)
  • Ripstop nylon for the wing skin (nearly weightless, gorgeous)
  • Wooden dowel for the body

This combo gives you the lightest wings with the most dramatic flap!

⚙️ Wing Lab: Set Pivot Type to match your hinge plan (pin, bolt, or living hinge) and Upper Attach if using elastic Open Wing Lab →
3

Build the Wing Frame

🔨
  • Bend bamboo or wire into your wing outline shape
  • Add ribs every 15-20 cm so the skin doesn't sag
  • Make both wings identical — lay one on top of the other to check

Symmetry matters! Take your time matching the pair.

📐 Wing Lab: Tap the Wing Shape button to pick your shape (Monarch, Swallowtail, etc). Adjust Wingspan and Chord sliders to match your frame Open Wing Lab →
4

Stretch & Attach Skin

🧵
  • Lay fabric over the frame — pull gently taut, no wrinkles
  • Hot glue or sew the skin to the frame edges
  • Trim excess, leaving 1 cm overlap

Don't stress about perfection — a little character adds charm!

5

Decorate!

🎨
  • Paint or decorate before attaching wings to the body
  • Real butterflies are symmetrical — match both sides
  • Let paint dry completely before moving on

This is the fun part! Make it yours. 🌈

6

Build the Body

🪵
  • Cut a dowel or tube to 15-25 cm
  • Drill a hole through the center for the wing hinge
  • Add a hook or loop at the top for the ceiling string

The body is just a connector — keep it simple!

⚖️ Wing Lab: Weigh your body piece and set the Body Weight slider. This affects your wing balance! Open Wing Lab →
7

Attach the Wings

📌
  • Push a wire or bolt through both wings and the body hole
  • Wings must swing freely — like a seesaw
  • Test: push one wingtip down. Does it swing smoothly? ✅

This is THE most important step. Free movement = beautiful flapping!

🔄 Wing Lab: Set Pivot Type to match your hinge. The Friction slider should be low for smooth flapping — check the Damping card! Open Wing Lab →
8

Get Your Numbers

🧮
  • Weigh your wings on a kitchen scale
  • Measure wingspan (tip to tip) and chord (front to back)
  • Enter numbers in Wing Lab — it tells you exactly how much weight to use

The calculator does the hard math. Trust it!

📸 Wing Lab: Enter your Wing Weight, Wingspan, Chord, and Tip Weight. The dashboard instantly shows your predicted flap speed, duration, and the Perfection Score! Open Wing Lab →
9

Add String & Weight

⚖️
  • Tie fishing line to each wing at the linkage point (Wing Lab tells you where)
  • Run both strings down through the body to the pull weight
  • Use the exact weight Wing Lab calculated — coins, washers, or clay work great

Almost there! One more step and you'll see it fly!

🎯 Wing Lab: Read the Pull Weight and Linkage Point % values — these tell you exactly how much weight and where to tie the strings on your wings Open Wing Lab →
10

Hang It Up & Watch It Fly!

🦋✨
  • Hang from the ceiling with a hook or strong tape
  • Give the weight a gentle tug and let go
  • Watch your butterfly flap beautifully!

Quick fixes if needed:

  • Barely moves? → Add more weight
  • Slams too hard? → Remove some weight
  • Too fast? → Add paperclips to wingtips
  • Only flaps once? → Lengthen the string
🎉🦋🎉

🛒 Wing Materials Guide

Pick the right material for your size. For large builds, you want a rigid frame + separate skin.

⭐ Recommended: 6-8 ft Gala Butterfly

Frame: Bamboo stakes or fiberglass rods · Skin: Ripstop nylon · Body: Wooden dowel

🌟 Budget-Friendly Alternative

Frame: Wooden dowels · Skin: Tissue paper (2 layers) · Body: Cardboard tube

Wing Surfaces (Skin)

🨁Ripstop Nylon3-8 ft

The gold standard. Nearly tear-proof, ultralight, stunning colors. Available at fabric stores.

✓ Lightest ✓ Tear-proof ✓ Beautiful drape Needs frame support
★ Our Pick for Gala
💎Organza / Sheer Fabric2-6 ft

Semi-transparent with a magical shimmer. Light passes through beautifully.

✓ Ethereal look ✓ Very light ✗ Tears easily
✨Mylar / Gift Wrap1-4 ft

Metallic shimmer, catches light beautifully. Lightweight but crinkles.

✓ Eye-catching ✓ Cheap ✗ Crinkly sound
📄Tissue Paper (2 layers)1-4 ft

Cheapest option. Layer 2 sheets for strength. Great for quick builds or kids.

✓ Ultralight ✓ $1 per sheet ✗ Fragile
📦Tyvek (house wrap)3-8 ft

Incredibly tough synthetic paper. Holds paint well, easy to cut. Free from construction sites!

✓ Indestructible ✓ Paintable Slightly stiffer

Rigid Panels (small builds without frame)

🟦Foam Board1-3 ft

Dollar store staple. Easy to cut, paint, and decorate. Perfect for small builds.

✓ Easy to cut ✓ Cheap ($1-2) ✗ Dents easily
📋Coroplast (corrugated plastic)2-4 ft

Like cardboard but waterproof. Campaign signs are made of this. Strong for its weight.

✓ Waterproof ✓ Very light ✗ Hard to curve
🪵Plywood (1/8" / 3mm)1-3 ft

Natural wood grain looks elegant. Laser-cut for precision or jigsaw by hand.

✓ Beautiful grain ✓ Strong ✗ Heavy Sand edges smooth!

Frame / Spar Materials

🎍Bamboo Stakes3-8 ft

Garden stakes are perfect wing spars. Strong, flexible, dirt cheap. Bundle 2-3 for the main spar.

✓ Strong & light ✓ $2-3 per 6-pack ✓ Flexible
★ Best Frame Material
🪵Wooden Dowels2-5 ft

Hardware store classic. 6mm (1/4") for ribs, 10mm (3/8") for main spars.

✓ Easy to find ✓ Easy to drill ✗ Doesn't bend
🎣Fiberglass Rods4-8 ft

Kite-building material. Incredibly light and flexible. Bends into curves without breaking.

✓ Lightest frame ✓ Bends perfectly ✗ Harder to find Kite supply shops
🔧PVC Pipe (thin wall)4-8 ft

1/2" PVC bends with a heat gun. Cheap and available everywhere.

✓ Very cheap ✓ Easy joins ✗ Heavier than bamboo
