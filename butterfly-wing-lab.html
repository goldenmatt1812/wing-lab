🧮 The Physics Behind the Flap

Real equations — this is exactly what the simulator computes

⚖️ Equation of Motion

Each wing pivots at the body. The angular acceleration d²θ/dt² comes from summing all torques and dividing by the moment of inertia:

Angular acceleration
I · θ̈ = τpendulum + τgravity + τspring + τelastic + τfriction + τair drag − c · θ̇

🔎 Torque Terms

Pull weight pendulum (nonlinear)
τpendulum = −mpull · g · rlink · sin(θ)
Net gravitational bias (string tension vs wing weight)
τgravity = τnet · cos(θ)
where τnet = T · rlink − (mwing · g · rcg + mtip · g · L)
and T = (mbody + mpull) · g / 2   (string tension per wing)
Air drag (quadratic, opposes motion)
τair = −2 · ½ρ Cd Awing rcp · v · |v|
where v = θ̇ · L/2 (tip velocity) and Cd = 1.2 (flat plate)

🛠️ Rest Angle

At equilibrium, the pendulum torque and gravitational bias cancel:

Static equilibrium
tan(θrest) = τnet / (mpull · g · rlink)

When τnet > 0 (heavy body/pull weight): wings tilt up (V-shape).
When τnet < 0 (heavy wings): wings droop down.

⚙️ Moment of Inertia

Total rotational inertia
I = 2(Iwing + Itip) + Ipull
Iwing = mwing · L² · (1+3λ) / (6(1+λ))   (λ = taper ratio)
Itip = mtip · L²   (point mass at tip)
Ipull = mpull · rlink²   (weight on linkage arm)

🎶 Natural Frequency

Linearized stiffness near rest
keff = mpull g rlink cos(θrest) + τnet sin(θrest) + kspring + kelastic
Damped natural frequency
ωn = √(keff / I)     fd = ωn√(1−ζ²) / 2π

💻 Numerical Integration

4th-order Runge-Kutta (RK4) at 120 Hz timestep. Captures nonlinear large-angle dynamics, air drag v² effects, and Coulomb friction transitions that closed-form solutions miss.

👁️ Perceptibility Analysis

Flap duration uses human motion perception thresholds at 3m viewing distance:

Displacement
≥ 5mm tip movement (visual acuity threshold at gala distance)
Velocity
≥ 5mm/s tip speed (motion detection threshold)

A flap is "visible" only when both thresholds are exceeded simultaneously.

ⓘ How to take a great wing photo

1. Lay the wing flat on a plain, contrasting surface. Press edges flat if they curl. Use dark paper under light wings, white paper under dark wings.

2. Place a reference object on the SAME flat surface (not overlapping the wing) so AI can measure real-world size:

📏 Ruler (best!) — place along the wing, markings visible
💳 Credit card — 8.56 × 5.40 cm
💵 Dollar bill — 15.6 × 6.6 cm
🪙 US quarter — 2.43 cm diameter
📄 Letter paper — lay wing ON it (27.9 × 21.6 cm)
📋 Sticky note — 7.6 × 7.6 cm

3. Lighting: Even, diffused light. No harsh shadows. Near a window is great. No flash!

4. Angle: Shoot straight down (bird's-eye). Keep your phone level and parallel to the table. Hold about 30–40 cm (12–16 in) above the wing.

5. Frame it: Get the whole wing + whole reference in the shot. Don't crop the edges.

You can photograph one wing or both — the AI handles either. Make sure your hand doesn't cast a shadow on the wing.
